Research

  • MyHand

    As part of the MyHand team, we are developing a wearable orthosis device for stroke survivors to assist in hand opening. The device is an underactuated, tendon-driven robot that is controlled via forearm surface EMG signals for ipsilateral control. Currently, I am working on surface EMG fabric sensors on the palm for improved intent inferral and designing finger splints to better support and assist finger extension.

    Relevant papers: Design and Development of Effective Transmission Mechanisms on a Tendon Driven Orthosis for Stroke Patients, Adaptive Semi-Supervised Intent Inferral to Control a Powered Hand Orthosis for Stroke

  • Edgeless Skin Actuation

    In collaboration with the Abaci Lab at Columbia Medical school, we are developing an actuated finger to introduce creases in artifically grown skin.

    Relevant papers: Engineering edgeless human skin with enhanced biomechanical properties
